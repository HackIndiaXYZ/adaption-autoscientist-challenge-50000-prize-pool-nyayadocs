# Known Limitations

- Most current training rows are synthetic or adapted examples; they are not a substitute for lawyer-reviewed field data.
- The independent benchmark is provisional until two reviewers confirm every label.
- Language coverage quality varies. Native-script and low-resource-language rows require fluent-speaker review.
- Rule-based OCR depends on image quality, document layout, Tesseract language packs, and spelling variation.
- Required certificate evidence and portal procedures vary by state and may change over time.
- CivicDocs prepares application packets but does not issue or approve certificates or benefits.
- ZamanatAI provides eligibility information and draft documents; courts retain full bail discretion.
- Groq and translation fallbacks may be unavailable, rate-limited, or return unexpected output.
- The current baseline evaluates module, intent, missing-document, JSON-validity, and safety metrics; entity F1 requires additional human-verified entity annotations.
- No accuracy claim should be published until measured on the locked, human-reviewed benchmark.
